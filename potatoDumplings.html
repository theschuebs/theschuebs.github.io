These Klöße (also known as Knödel) make a lecker side for Sauerbraten, Rouladen, and many more...

INGREDIENTS

  • One box Bavarian Potato Dumpling mix, Panni makes a great one
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt
  • Previously made bread crumbs

TIPS AND TRICKS

  • The crutons are the secret ingredient in my family. We cube day old bread and sauté in brown butter (lots of it!)
  • Be careful. If the dumpling is too thin around the crutons, boiling will cause the dumpling to open up.
  • It's easier to roll the dumplings if you have wet hands.

Kartoffelklöße

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Take package contents and stir into 2 cups cold water until smooth. Let mixture stand about 10 minutes to thicken.
  2. Take mixture and form into balls (about 2 inches in diameter). Place two or three crutons in the center and roll into a solid ball, trying to make sure the crutons are not visible.
  3. Place dumplings into a large pot of boiling salted water. Cook over medium heat, uncovered, for about 20 minutes.
  4. Remove from water and serve hot. Makes around 8 dumplings.
